# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

BurnLink is committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We are dedicated to creating an environment where everyone feels respected, valued, and able to contribute meaningfully, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, political belief, profession, race, religion, sexual identity and orientation, or socioeconomic status.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to creating a positive environment include:

- **Being respectful** — Use inclusive language, listen actively to others' perspectives
- **Providing constructive feedback** — Focus on ideas, not individuals; be kind and thoughtful
- **Acknowledging mistakes** — Admit when you're wrong and learn from feedback
- **Respecting boundaries** — Understand that people have different comfort levels and preferences
- **Celebrating diversity** — Value different backgrounds, experiences, and viewpoints
- **Focusing on what's best for the community** — Help others succeed and grow

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Offensive comments related to gender, sexual orientation, race, religion, or disability
- Deliberate intimidation or threats
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting comments, or personal attacks
- Publishing private information without consent
- Spamming, advertising, or solicitation
- Any behavior that violates the law or puts others at risk

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All project spaces (GitHub, issues, pull requests, discussions)
- Community communications (Discord, forums, email lists)
- Project events, conferences, and meetups
- Interactions when representing the project publicly

## Enforcement

Community members who witness violations are encouraged to report them to the project maintainers.

### Reporting

If you experience or witness a violation, please report it by:

1. **Emailing the project maintainers** with details of the incident
2. **Opening a private discussion** on GitHub if you feel more comfortable
3. **Contacting a community moderator** if one is designated

All reports will be:
- Reviewed confidentially
- Treated seriously and investigated fairly
- Handled with discretion to protect everyone's privacy

### Consequences

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in:

- A warning or request to modify behavior
- Temporary or permanent removal from project spaces
- Revocation of contributor privileges
- Public disclosure (at maintainers' discretion) if the violation is severe

The goal is to educate and improve, not to punish. We believe in second chances and growth.
